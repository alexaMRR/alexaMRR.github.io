What is Data Sovereignty?

Data Sovereignty is the idea that data, which is in essence information, should be maintained and controlled by those from who and where it’s collected– and in turn, they have power over how it’s interpreted and distributed. The National Library of Medicine describes data sovereignty as “a group or individual’s right to control and maintain their own data, which includes the collection, storage, and interpretation of data”
